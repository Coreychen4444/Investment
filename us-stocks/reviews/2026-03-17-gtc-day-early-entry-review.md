# Review — Catalyst Day Early Entry

## Summary
Entered multiple positions too early around a major industry keynote. The long-term AI infra thesis was not the problem. The problem was treating early-session strength as confirmation and using high-beta names as if they were hard-core event winners.

## Verified facts
- Major keynote scheduled for afternoon session
- STOCK_A (optics): opened strong, sold off ~10% from high to close
- STOCK_B (optics): opened strong, sold off ~8% from high to close

## Main mistake
- Bought before the market finished digesting the event
- Confused long-term thesis strengthening with same-day price continuation
- Used high-beta optics names as if they were the cleanest event expression

## Specific lesson
STOCK_A later did not need to be panic-sold, but the original entry was still structurally inferior to a later confirmed entry ~6% lower.

## Rules extracted
- Early strength on catalyst days is not confirmation.
- Separate core thesis beneficiaries from high-beta sentiment beneficiaries.
- Better confirmation at a worse-looking price is preferable to a prettier early entry.
