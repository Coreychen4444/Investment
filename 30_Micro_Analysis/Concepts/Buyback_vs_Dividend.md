# 资本配置：回购 vs 分红 (Capital Allocation)

**Core Principle**: Capital allocation is the CEO's most important job. How management deploys retained earnings determines long-term shareholder value.

## 1. Why Buybacks (General)

Stock buybacks (Share Repurchases) have become the dominant form of returning capital in the modern US market.

- **Flexibility**: Buybacks can be stopped or started at any time without market panic. In bad times, companies can conserve cash. Dividends, once established, are "sticky"—cutting them is seen as a sign of distress and punishes the stock price.
- **Undervaluation Signal**: When a company buys back its own stock below intrinsic value, it creates immediate value for remaining shareholders. It's effectively buying $1 of value for $0.80.
- **Tax Efficiency (Universal)**: Buybacks do not trigger an immediate tax event for shareholders. You only pay taxes when you choose to sell your shares (Capital Gains). Dividends are "forced" taxable events for all shareholders in the year they are paid.

## 2. Why Dividends (General)

- **Tangible Return**: Cash in hand is real. It provides a steady income stream for investors who need liquidity.
- **Discipline**: Committing to a regular dividend forces management to be disciplined with cash flow. It prevents "empire building" or wasting cash on low-return acquisitions, as the cash literally "leaves the building."

## 3. The Math of Value Creation

When a company repurchases shares:
1. **Shares Outstanding** decreases.
2. **Net Income** remains the same (assuming cash used wasn't generating high returns).
3. **EPS (Earnings Per Share)** increases.

$$ \text{EPS} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Shares Outstanding}} $$

Even if the business creates zero extra profit, a 5% reduction in share count boosts EPS by ~5.3%.

> [!NOTE] Bonus for SG Investors
> For Singapore-based investors, buybacks have an even stronger mathematical edge due to the tax treaty (or lack thereof) with the US:
> - **Capital Gains Tax**: **0%**. Gains from price appreciation (driven by buybacks) are tax-free.
> - **Dividend Withholding Tax**: **30%**. Dividends paid by US companies are taxed at 30% before they reach you.
>
> Therefore, **$1 of Buyback > $1 of Dividend** for an SG investor.
