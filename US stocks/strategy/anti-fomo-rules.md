# Anti-FOMO Trading Rules

## Core diagnosis
The problem is not lack of logic. The problem is trying to convert a good logic into the prettiest possible price.

## The impossible trio
Do not try to get all three:
1. Buy the lowest
2. Sell the highest
3. Capture the full move

This trio does not exist in repeatable real trading.

## Common failure modes
- **FOMO entry**: "If I don't buy now, I'll miss the low."
- **Greedy hold**: "If I sell now, I'll earn less than I could."
- **Fear exit**: "If I don't act now, the loss will get worse."

## Replacement rules
1. **Confirmation has a price. Pay it.**
   A worse price for better confirmation is not failure.
   Example: a confirmed ~$600 LITE entry after the market finishes digesting GTC is structurally better than an early ~$638 entry taken before confirmation.

2. **Missing part of a move is acceptable.**
   Missing a segment is better than forcing a bad entry.

3. **No confirmation = early entry.**
   If the market has not finished confirming, label the trade honestly as a risky early entry.

4. **Do not optimize for the prettiest trade.**
   Optimize for repeatability, survivability, and decent odds.

5. **Less profit is not a loss.**
   Small or partial wins are still wins; undisciplined trading is what destroys compounding.
