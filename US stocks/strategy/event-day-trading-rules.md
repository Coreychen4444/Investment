# Event-Day / Earnings-Day Trading Rules

## Purpose
Rules for trading around high-expectation catalyst days such as:
- GTC
- earnings
- product launches
- keynote conferences

## Iron Rules
1. **Early strength is not confirmation.**
   Opening strength, premarket strength, or first-half momentum does not mean the market has finished pricing the event.

2. **Wait for digestion before sizing high-beta subthemes.**
   If trading high-beta names tied to a catalyst (e.g. optics/transceivers around GTC), do not size aggressively before the market has digested the content.

3. **Separate core winners from sentiment expressions.**
   Example framework:
   - Core / harder-expression beneficiaries
   - High-beta / sentiment-expression beneficiaries
   Do not size them the same.

4. **Thesis validation != same-day upside.**
   A catalyst can strengthen long-term logic while triggering same-day sell-the-news in crowded subthemes.

5. **Do not turn a decent thesis into an early oversized trade.**
   The recurring execution risk is entering before the market finishes its real vote.

## Checklist Before Trading a Catalyst Day
- Is this move driven by pre-event expectation or post-event digestion?
- Am I buying the hardest expression of the theme, or the highest-beta expression?
- If the catalyst is merely "good, not shocking," does this stock still need to go up today?
- What happens if the market validates the thesis but rotates out of the subtheme?
- Am I treating first-half action as if the full day already confirmed me?
