# Trading Rules v1

## Core purpose
Protect against FOMO, early entry, one-size-fits-all actions, and confusing correct logic with correct timing.

## Entry rules
1. Every trade must be labeled as one of:
   - Confirmed trade
   - Probe trade
   - Early/risky trade
   If you cannot label it clearly, do not trade.

2. No confirmation = no size.
   Unconfirmed trades are probe trades only.

3. High-expectation catalyst days (GTC, earnings, keynotes): early strength is not confirmation.

4. Before entry, answer:
   - What type of trade is this?
   - What is the catalyst?
   - What proves me wrong?
   - Where do I reduce/exit if wrong?

5. Same thesis: maximum 2 entries total.
   No third add.

## Exit rules
6. Exits are for one of three reasons:
   - Thesis/event has been priced
   - Structure has weakened
   - Time window ended

7. Weak rebound is not proof of recovery.
   It may be a risk-reduction window.

8. If timing was wrong, reduce risk before arguing with the market.

## Position rules
9. Size must match certainty.
   High-beta names cannot be handled like core thesis names.

10. Separate core expressions from sentiment expressions.

## Anti-FOMO rules
11. Confirmation matters more than pretty prices.
12. Buying a bit higher for stronger confirmation is acceptable.
13. Missing part of a move is not failure.

## Four iron rules
- No confirmation, no size.
- Max two entries per thesis.
- Early strength on catalyst days is not confirmation.
- If you are chasing the prettiest price, stop.
